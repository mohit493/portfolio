Hello Google UX Research team,

  • Sample UX research study design

A key part for the UX Researcher Intern position- “is to support designing different types of research studies”. Hence, to give a better context and understanding to my thought process I thought of walking through my thought process while designing a sample study related to Large advertisers for Google (just as an example).

Assumptions

For the sake of the study, I assume Large Advertisers are defined as user accounts (single or a group of them related to a single organization) that spend $5000/month for at least a period of 12 months (assumption) on Google's advertising products.

As there would be some product usage data (or logs), hence assuming that digital footprint about the demographics, user engagement (frequency and recency) and user loyalty (repeating sessions) would be available. Hence I would think to choose more in-field methods to gather valuable insights.

Also assuming before the start of the study a through analysis has been done in the existing knowledge and the company’s research database to validate the knowledge gap.

Research question

Regarding the focus of the study, the research question could be chosen to understand different aspects of the experience. For example- “How do Large Advertisers hear about the Google’s Advertising products?” Or “What goals (Jobs to be done) do large advertisers wish to fulfill via the Google’s advertising products?”, etc.

However, in the scope of this exercise with limited information, I choose my research question to be- “Why do Large Advertisers stop using Google’s advertising products?”.

The advantage of this question is that it could help identify some relevant pain points of the target users from studying the extreme users (for the sake of this case study). These pain points could expose the variety of other parameters (and stakeholders) to consider while designing a more frictionless user-centric advertising products.

Approach

>Identifying the sample (Sampling)
This is extremely crucial especially in this case, as the decision makers on the choice of the advertising platform and the users actually using the platform might not be the same. Hence, it's suggested to sample people who make the decision on the choice of advertising platforms, the people who define the return on investment for advertising strategy(for understanding relevant metrics); along with the actual users of the product.

>Understand the perceived usability of Google advertising products; by the users
Conduct a usability study with the participant performing the major tasks (as perceived by the product team). Post-study usability evaluation via SUPR-Q questionnaire. SUPR-Q is suggested in comparison to SUS and WAMMI; as it has components to measure trust, credibility, and loyalty. This could give an idea about the sample’s perceived relationship to the product.

>Gather some qualitative data
Semi-structured interviews preferably in the participant’s office environment. The suggested themes for the interview:

  • To understand the decision-making process(and identify major stakeholders) for choosing the platform.
  • To understand the expected value from the product by the sample.

Semi-structured interviews are preferred as they give openness to learn something new from the participant and at the same time covering the basic themes structuring the interview.

>Ethnography
A field study of the sample’s work environment would be highly valuable. It could provide a rich qualitative data providing context and meaning to quantitative data. A key focus during the field trip:

  • Office seating arrangement (open or close; could give context to decision making structure in the organization).
  • The type of technology present in the work environment (especially with decision makers and target users). This could give us some context into the priority of digital promotion in the company.

Deliverables

The deliverable of the insights from the research could be in many forms- presentation, images or even bullet point in an email; depending on the team the insight to be conveyed to. Based on my judgment (in this context) however, the best form of common communication tool could be a set of User Personas. It could be used by the product manager, engineers and designers to base their product decisions on.


PS: It’s important to note that this is just one extreme of the scale and product decisions should not be made solely on this research. It could be biasing. Hence the users on the other spectrum- the most loyal/engaged/hooked users should also be studied to make balanced confident decisions.
