Hello Google, I am Mohit.

  • Google Cover Letter

I come from a UX design background and specialize in finding meaningful patterns in people's thinking while interacting with technology. Over the course of my professional journey, I have got the opportunity to live (between 2-13 months) in 5 different countries and work with a variety of professionals- developers, fellow designers, researchers and even politicians. I believe these cultural exposures and the experiences of interacting with different types of stakeholders allow me to collaborate effectively within teams.

Why am I interested in becoming a UX researcher?

During my 2013 internship, I got the chance to use design methods like card sorting, cognitive walkthrough, and stakeholder interviews while redesigning a software. I was amazed by the magical effectiveness these simple methods have in uncovering significant differences in the way software was developed and how the users think? My masters provided with the opportunity to learn and practice more traditional research techniques like lab studies, surveys, and correlation studies. With the knowledge of these realms, I found myself curious to figure out the Why? and What? behind user’s interactions. At present, I find myself comfortable in creating the right amalgamation of design methods to suit a particular problem area and would like to use this knowledge to work on more global products. As a UX researcher, I see my role in revealing and communicating user insights in a way that would allow engineers, designers and product managers to make more confident product decisions.

Why Google?

Working in a company like Google would allow me to contribute to teams building digital products that shape daily interactions in millions of lives. As Google has varied projects like Android (with global user base) or Google station in India (with a more local user base); it would provide me a challenge to work with a variety of user types in different contexts. The opportunity to work on these varied challenges would significantly boost my growth as a UX researcher. On top of this, it’s an opportunity to work and learn in an environment of highly talented and motivated people.

I hope from my portfolio, cv and the cover letter; I can accurately convey my competencies and motivation for this role. I would love to get a chance to contribute in building products that are shaping the new ways of living. Lastly if available, I would also be open to being considered for UX Researcher position at Google-Zurich office; if any. :)
