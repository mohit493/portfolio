About me

I am an Experience Designer with a hybrid educational background in design, ICT, and entrepreneurship. I use design as a tool to chisel out a digital product that acts as a natural extension to a user’s needs. In the process of my work, I try to adhere to the following three principles-

  • Making design choices with honesty: Remove personal biases, team egos, and irrationality.
  • Persistence: Continuous focus on the present stage of design with overall vision in mind.
  • Clear communication: Avoid fancy jargons while conveying the design process and findings.

Over the past five years, I have worked with government ministries, research institutes, and start-ups in following domains-

Online neighbourhoods, Internet of things, Event management, Energy management and Instant messaging

Currently, I am looking for interesting opportunities to work as a UX researcher to reveal and design around the real needs of the target users.

EATUP.SE- Product design case study

EATUP.SE

Product design case study

  • Duration: January 2016 - May 2016
  • Team: Daniel Delgado, Daniel Meusburger, Giovanna Vilaza, Mohit Yadav, Mikko Honkanen, Sritam Paltasingh and Vamshi Muga

As part of our Business Development Lab course, our team developed Eatup.se; an online marketplace designed for restaurants and cafes in Stockholm to advertise meal deals of their excess food to students looking for affordable and healthy meals. At the end of the project, we had 4 partner restaurants, 3 partner organizations, 186 potential customers on our subscription list and a functional online MVP.

My role in the journey

As a product designer, my role focussed on uncovering the user’s needs and shaping the product experience to meet them. To do that I worked on an idea validation survey, prototype development & testing of the initial concept and played a major role in the brand design and devoloping the promotional strategy for the product.

Business development process

Customer-centric business development

Our team followed a customer-centric business development process. We involved our targeted customers right from problem validation to product launch. We surveyed 69 potential customers and talked to 20-30 restaurants and cafes to verify the problem we were trying to address. This research helped us to narrow our target demographics and gave an estimate for setting the price point for the meal deals.

We also conducted a scenario based mid-fidelity prototype testing with 5 potential customers. The goal of the test was to understand the mental model of the user when they are searching for meal deals (like preferences about price, location or meal type). The outcome of the study resulted in several interface level changes like ->food filter, showing money being saved and prioritizing price rather than location.

In hindsight, these insights are quite obvious, but it was the user-testing that was able to uncover these real needs.

Meaningful partnerships and mentors

Partnerships

Our marketplace had a consumer side who buy the meal deals and a seller side who sell the meal deals on the platform. To have a relevant database we tried to forge meaningful partnerships on both sides. Our team partnered with 4 restaurants in Stockholm agreeing to sell their meal deals on our platform, 1 Stockholm university student organization, 1 food sharing facebook community and an existing lunch box sharing startup in Stockholm.

Mentorships

Mentors were essential in our business development process as they were able to provide an experienced outside perspective and add a meaningful dimension to the product. We had a constant support from our course coordinator, and coach Dr. Terrence Brown (Associate Professor, KTH), Aurore Belfrage (Co-founder of Wrapp) guided us on our business strategy and Felipe Morales Macowan (Fjord, Stockholm) mentored on designing our end-to-end marketplace service experience.

Product promotion and launch

We designed our campaign around our target demographics of 20-35 age group. We used Facebook as our online medium of promotion; along with poster branding at SU and KTH university campuses. We also participated in student organized events to promote our platform. The goal of promotion was to get potential customers excited about to product and generate interest for the launch. (186 people subscribe on the pre-launch page).

For our initial MVP launch, our team coordinated a picnic with our partners at Stockholm university on 20th May 2016. The goal was to spread awareness about our partners, showcase our MVP and also give a chance to the users to try out the food from the partner restaurants. At the launch event venue, we received a gathering of 120+ potential-customers, making it a success.

Personal reflection on the journey

Over the course of the startup journey, I realized a startup's biggest asset is the team. During the process of development, a lot of dimensions defined and it’s the team's skills & motivations that keep the business development moving forward. Our team consisted of designers, developers, and marketing people, each having a definite role based on a common core value proposition. Due to the commitment of the team, we were able to forge 7 meaningful partnerships, develop an MVP and receive 186 potential-customers on our mailing list.

Key learnings from the project-

  • Involve customers early- Build and test the product regularly with the actual customers, helps in producing a more tailored product.
  • Show rather than tell- Design an early prototype; it’s easier to receive feedback on something tangible. It's also simpler to convey the product vision to mentors and partners.
  • Meaningful partnerships- Partners and mentors could add an important dimension to the product. So it's essential to develop meaningful business relationships.

As all our team members were part of a European double-masters program, we had to move to different universities (in different countries) in the year after. As the product was heavily dependent on the local partners; hence we sold our product(and the relationships) to one of our competitor ResQ-club.

EVENTFORTE- Event management platform

EVENTFORTE

Event management platform

EventForte is working on creating cloud based tools for easily managing Events. As an UX consultant my primary task was to help design a virtual event management tool (developed using Unity) for the Event organisers, Event attendees and Hotels(designed specifically for each user). Following section shows primarily the tool designed for the event organisers.

  • Duration: January 2015 - June 2015
  • Freelance Project
  • www.eventforte.com

Event Diagramming

The tool helps event organisers to create the whole Event in a virtual environment. This gives the organiser the freedom to customize and iterate the events easily. Eventforte algorithms also provides suggestions for stage placement (based on the placement of audience), furniture layout based on number of people and the event layout, etc.

Guest Manager

The guest manager allows organiser to manage a guest list. Assign them custom tags, assign seatings and also provide personalized service based on a particualar guest's requirement. This section allowed customisation to the granular level; from arranging seating in individual event section to inviting to an attendees to just a part of the event (sub-event).

Check-IN

The check-IN section allows one swipe checkin and checkout of guests. It also allows organizers to make any last minute changes based on a request made by a guest.

The sections discussed above are primarily for the Event Organisers. The tools also has sections for Hotel Managers and Event Attendees.

For more information visit www.eventforte.com

TENER- Software redesign project

Tener

Software redesign project

Tener is a cloud based energy management tool used to monitor and manage energy consumption in several municipalities of Switzerland. As UX researcher my aim was to understand different users' (Municipal Officer's, Janitors, Engineers and General) goals, attitudes and behaviours. As a deliverable I presesented a redesigned prototype of the tool based on the findings from user research.

  • Duration: May 2013 - July 2013
  • Internship Insitute : Institute Of Information Systems, HES-SO
  • Mentor: Prof. Alexandre Cotting

Re-Design Methodology

In order to make the tool more intuitive User Centered Design Methodology was adopted. The primary step was to conduct a set of Usability tests (User Interviews, Card Sorting, Cognitive Walkthrough and Heuristic Evaluation). The tests provided us with a set of problems in the overall usability of the tool. A functional prototype was created showcasing a set of possible solutions to the usability issue. Lastly visuals of the tool were created to give it a more refined identity.

  • 1. Research
  • 2. Synthesis
  • 3. Design

User Interviews

3 different types of target users were interviewed to get an insight into the problems faced by them.

  • Mr. Arnaud Zufferey (Ex-City Municipal | Monitors Energy Consumption | Uses on Regular Basis)
  • Mr. Floret Jean-Claude (Janitor | Enter Data into Tener | Once Every Day)
  • Mr. Monnet (Electrician | Analyze sensor data | Once a week)

Card Sorting and Cognitive Walkthrough

Card sorting and cognitive walkthrough activity were conducted in order to understand the gap between the mental modals of the users.

Though the sample size was not big enough to draw direct conclusions but it highlighted the few major usability issues.

Heuristic Evaluation

The tool's usability was evaluated based on Nielsen's Design Heuristics .

Key issues in the usability were related to the Proper visual consistency, Proper system feedback and aesthetics of the tool.

Key Identified Problems

Different set of users have very different expectation from the tool.

Make the tool more specific based on the LogIn Id's of the user.

The software's visual are not appealing.

Make the software more consistent. Use grids for proper aligning, icons for global communication and use colors to create visual hierarchy.

The navigation structure of the tool is very complex for the user.

Proper grouping of the features under similar cateogory and making is upfront visual for quick navigation.

The translation between language of the tool is not very clear.

Use more icons that are universal to understand.

App Visuals

One of the key focus area of the redesign process was the software visual. Manily because several users termed the software to be unappealing, unattractive and visually outdated. Our main goal was to make the tool structured, clean and visually consistent.

The main focus during the visual redesign were :

  • Grid Structure - to make the tool visually consistent.
  • Color Scheme and font sizes - to create visual hierarchy
  • Icons - Easy understanding of the functions in all the different languages
